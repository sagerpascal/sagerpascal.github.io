About Me


Greetings and welcome to my personal homepage. I am Pascal Sager, specializing in the captivating domain of AI and deep learning. Currently pursuing a Ph.D. degree with a focus on Natural Intelligence, my academic foundation spans electrical engineering, computer science, and data science. I work as a researcher, project leader, and Head of AI demonstrators at the Centre for Artificial Intelligence of the Zurich University of Applied Sciences and am a visiting researcher at the Institute of Neuroinformatics of the University of Zurich and ETH Zurich.
Beyond the technical realm, my commitment to responsible technology is evident through my role as a Board member in the university's Sustainable Impact Program. Additionally, I contribute my expertise as a Senior Data Scientist at AlpineAI, actively participating in the development of SwissGPT.


In the realm of education, I instruct various courses at ZHAW, including AI labs, machine learning, and data mining labs, while co-supervising projects, bachelor theses, and master theses. Furthermore, I showcase AI capabilities at diverse events and deliver talks at notable gatherings such as the Annual Congress of the Swiss Academy of Technical Sciences, the Green Bytes Event organized by DIZH, ZHAW's Night of Technology, and Zurich's Scientifica. I am actively engaged in networks like the Rising Researcher Network of CLAIRE, the Data Innovation Alliance, and the Digital Future Lab, I continue to contribute to the field.


The next chapter of my academic journey holds promise as my doctoral studies delve into the intricacies of natural intelligence, combining my fascination with neuroscience and the potential offered by artificial intelligence. This endeavor aims to unravel the mysteries of intelligence in living organisms and apply these insights to advance AI systems.
